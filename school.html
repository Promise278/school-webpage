Formal

Knowledge dissemination: The primary aim is to equip students with the theoretical and practical knowledge that is essential for personal development and societal welfare. Formal education is based on a set curriculum. Formal education encompasses primary, lower and upper secondary education, higher and university education that culminate in the achievement of a degree or a professional qualification or diploma or a recognised certification as well as adult education programmes.

In-Formal

Informal education aims to establish or develop environments in which people can learn, investigate and expand their experiences, as well as implement modifications. Establishes an environment that allows individuals to understand and together develop of each other. After joining the school or even without schooling, an individual learns many things through peer groups, friends, television, films, radio etc. This learning takes place by observation and imitation (copy of something) in an informal way. Hence, it is called as Informal or Incidental Education.

Non-formal learning has experiential learning activities that foster the development of skills and knowledge. This helps in building the confidence and abilities among the youth of today.
